Our Product

EcoForm Olive™ is a sustainable packaging solution developed by transforming discarded olive pits into a flexible, protective, and compostable material. Our product is designed to replace plastic fillers and other unsustainable packaging alternatives commonly used in shipping and storage.

By utilising agricultural waste, EcoForm Olive™ addresses two key environmental challenges: excessive plastic usage and food-industry by-product waste. The result is a material that maintains functionality while significantly reducing environmental impact.

  • Plastic-free and fully compostable
  • Produced from recycled olive pit waste
  • Lightweight yet effective for protection
  • Reduces landfill waste and CO₂ emissions
  • Designed for modern, sustainable supply chains

Why EcoForm Olive™?

EcoForm Olive™ was created to demonstrate how innovation and sustainability can work together. Our product supports businesses seeking environmentally responsible packaging solutions, while encouraging the reuse of natural resources that would otherwise be discarded. This approach reflects modern economic thinking focused on circularity, efficiency, and long-term value.

Our approach reflects modern economic thinking centred on circular economy principles, where materials are reused, repurposed, and kept in circulation for as long as possible.

Sustainability Impact

EcoForm Olive™ contributes to a more sustainable future by directly addressing two major environmental challenges: plastic pollution and agricultural waste. Conventional packaging materials often rely on single-use plastics, which require large amounts of fossil fuels to produce and can take hundreds of years to decompose. Our solution offers a responsible alternative that reduces reliance on these harmful materials.

By utilising olive pits — a natural by-product of the food and olive oil industry — EcoForm Olive™ transforms waste that would otherwise be discarded into a valuable resource. This process supports responsible resource management and promotes the reuse of materials already available within Cyprus, reducing the need for additional raw material extraction.
